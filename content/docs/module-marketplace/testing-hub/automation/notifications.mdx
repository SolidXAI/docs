Notifications help your team respond quickly without watching the dashboard all day. For each product, decide who should receive results and which results matter to them.

## A practical approach

- Send **needs attention** notifications to the product owner and the team responsible for the affected journey.
- Send successful results only when they support a release decision or regular business review.
- Keep the recipient list current as responsibilities change.

## What a useful notification tells you

A notification should help you answer three questions quickly:

1. Which product was checked?
2. Is a customer journey affected?
3. Where can I open the full result and share it with the team?

<Callout type="tip" title="Avoid alert fatigue">
  Notifications are most valuable when they lead to action. Start with alerts for journeys that need attention, then add successful-result notifications only where they add value.
</Callout>
